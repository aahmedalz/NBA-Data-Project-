Task Updates
Data Setup – Completed
The data setup phase has been successfully completed, establishing a strong foundation for the remainder of the project. During this stage, we focused not only on acquiring the datasets but also on ensuring their reliability and reproducibility. Both datasets (team_stats_traditional_rs.csv and team_stats_advanced_rs.csv) were downloaded from the NBA statistics repository, and their integrity was verified using SHA-256 hashing. This step is critical because it guarantees that the datasets remain unchanged across different stages of development and between team members, preventing inconsistencies that could compromise results.
In addition to data verification, we created a well-structured GitHub repository to organize all project components. The repository includes clearly defined directories for raw data, processed data, scripts, notebooks, and documentation. This structure promotes collaboration and ensures that all team members can efficiently navigate and contribute to the project.
We also developed initial Python scripts using Pandas to load, inspect, and summarize the datasets. These scripts include checks for missing values, data types, and basic descriptive statistics. Early inspection allowed us to identify potential inconsistencies in column naming conventions and data formatting, which will be addressed during the data cleaning phase. Overall, this stage ensures that our workflow is reproducible, organized, and ready for more advanced processing.
Data Join – In Progress
We have begun merging the traditional and advanced datasets using a multi-key join on TEAM_ID and SEASON. This step is crucial because it combines complementary perspectives on team performance into a single dataset that can be used for analysis and modeling.
Our approach involves performing an inner join using Pandas, ensuring that only teams with matching entries in both datasets are included. After the merge, we are conducting validation checks such as comparing row counts before and after the join, verifying that no unexpected duplicates are introduced, and examining null values in key columns.
We are also implementing additional validation steps, including spot-checking specific teams and seasons to confirm that the merged values align correctly. If discrepancies arise, we plan to trace them back to their source and determine whether they are due to missing data, naming inconsistencies, or structural differences between datasets. This phase is being led by Ahmed and is essential for ensuring the accuracy of all downstream analysis.
Exploratory Data Analysis – Starting Soon
Once the datasets are successfully merged and cleaned, we will begin the exploratory data analysis phase. This stage will provide insights into relationships between traditional and advanced metrics and guide our feature selection process.
Our analysis will include generating a correlation matrix to identify relationships between variables, particularly focusing on how advanced metrics correlate with traditional statistics and win percentage. We will also conduct distribution analysis to understand the spread and skewness of key variables, which may inform transformations such as normalization or log scaling.
Additionally, we will assess multicollinearity among features, as highly correlated variables can negatively impact model performance and interpretability. Visualization techniques such as heatmaps, histograms, and scatter plots will be used extensively to uncover patterns and trends. This phase, led by Will, will play a critical role in shaping the modeling strategy.
Modeling – Not Started
The modeling phase will involve training a Random Forest Regressor to predict team win percentage. Random Forest is well-suited for this task due to its ability to handle nonlinear relationships, reduce overfitting through ensemble learning, and provide feature importance metrics.
We plan to split the dataset into training and testing sets and evaluate model performance using metrics such as Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE). Hyperparameter tuning will also be performed to optimize model performance. Additionally, feature importance outputs will be analyzed to identify which statistics contribute most significantly to predicting team success.

Changes to Project Plan
Data Handling
We are considering excluding seasons that were shortened due to lockouts, such as 2011–12 and 2019–20, to maintain consistency in our analysis. Alternatively, we may convert win totals into win percentages, which would allow us to retain these seasons while standardizing comparisons across different season lengths. This decision will be finalized during the exploratory data analysis phase.
Feature Engineering
We anticipate the need to standardize or normalize features, particularly because NBA gameplay has evolved significantly over time. Differences in pace, scoring trends, and shot selection could introduce bias if not properly accounted for. To address this, we are considering incorporating pace-adjusted statistics and per-100 possession metrics. These adjustments will help ensure that comparisons across eras are meaningful and that the model captures underlying performance rather than stylistic differences.
Challenges So Far
One of the primary challenges we have encountered is data consistency across different eras of NBA basketball. Changes in rules and playing styles make direct comparisons between older and modern statistics difficult. Our solution is to rely more heavily on rate-based and relative metrics.
Another challenge involves handling lockout seasons, which may distort win totals. As discussed, we are evaluating whether to exclude these seasons or standardize the target variable.
High dimensionality is also a concern, as the merged dataset is expected to contain over 50 features. This increases the risk of overfitting and complicates model interpretation. To address this, we plan to use feature importance analysis, dimensionality reduction techniques, and correlation-based feature selection.
Finally, data integration presents potential risks, particularly in ensuring that the merge process does not introduce errors. We are mitigating this by implementing thorough validation checks and investigating any anomalies that arise.
